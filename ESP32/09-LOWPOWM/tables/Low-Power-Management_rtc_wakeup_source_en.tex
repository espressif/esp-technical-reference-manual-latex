        \item[3] EXT1 is especially designed to wake up the chip from any sleep mode, and it also supports multiple pads' combinations. \\First, \hyperref[regdesc:RTCCNTLEXTWAKEUP1REG]{RTC\_CNTL\_EXT\_WAKEUP1\_SEL[17:0]} should be configured with the bitmap of PADS selected as a wake-up source. However, if \hyperref[regdesc:RTCCNTLEXTWAKEUPCONFREG]{RTC\_CNTL\_EXT\_WAKEUP1\_LV} is 0, it needs all selected PADs to be at low-voltage level to trigger a wake-up.

        {\bfseries Note} that the EXT1 hold time should be longer than three RTC slow clock cycles, otherwise the signal status will not be captured in \hyperref[regdesc:RTCCNTLEXTWAKEUP1STATUSREG]{RTC\_CNTL\_EXT\_WAKEUP1\_STATUS}.

        \item[5] Wake-up is triggered by receiving any SDIO command.

        \item [7] Wake-up is triggered when the number or positive edges of RxD signal is greater than or equal to (\hyperref[UARTACTIVETHRESHOLD]{UART\_ACTIVE\_THRESHOLD}+2). {\bfseries Note} that the RxD signal cannot be input through GPIO Matrix but only through IO\_MUX.
