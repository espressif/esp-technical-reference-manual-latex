\chapter{ADC Controller (ADC)}
\label{mod:adc}
\section{Overview}

\chipname{} integrates two 12-bit successive approximation ADCs (SAR ADCs) for measuring analog signals from up to 14 pins. Figure \ref{figure:sar adc overview} shows the basic architecture of the SAR ADC module on \chipname{}.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=1\textwidth]{63-ADC/figures/SAR_ADC_simplified_architecture.pdf}
    \caption{SAR ADC Simplified Architecture}
    \label{figure:sar adc overview}
\end{figure}

As the figure shows, the SAR ADCs are managed by four dedicated controllers:

\begin{itemize}
   \item Two HP (high-performance) controllers: HP ADC1 Controller and HP ADC2 Controller, designed for multi-channel sampling mode, supporting continuous transfer of conversion results to memory via the GDMA interface.
   \item Two LP (low-power) controllers: LP ADC1 Controller and LP ADC2 Controller, supporting operation in sleep mode and one-shot sampling mode.
\end{itemize}

\section{Terminology}

The following terms related to SAR ADC are defined in the context of the \textit{\chipname{} Technical Reference Manual} to help readers better understand this document:

\begin{longtable}{ R{6cm} p{10cm} }
\textbf{SAR ADC:} & Including analog ADC circuit and digital ADC controller. The HP ADC and LP ADC in the following text share the analog ADC circuit, but have their own controllers, namely the HP ADC controllers and LP ADC controllers. The SAR ADC mentioned in the text refers to both HP ADC and LP ADC.\\
\textbf{HP ADC:} & High-performance ADC, supporting multi-channel sampling, GDMA data transfer, etc., not available in sleep mode.\\
\textbf{LP ADC:} & Low-power ADC, supporting one-shot sampling mode, can be used in sleep mode.\\
\textbf{One-shot sampling mode:} & In this mode, LP ADC samples one channel at a time. \\
\textbf{Multi-channel sampling mode:} & In this mode, HP ADC sequentially samples a group of channels or continuously samples a single channel.  \\
\textbf{Dual HP ADC sampling:} & It means two HP ADCs sample either simultaneously or alternatively. \\
\textbf{Conversion result:} & The conversion result is the binary digital value obtained after the analog-to-digital conversion process. It is sometimes written as conversion data.\\
\textbf{Filtered data:} & Filtered data is the result of processing conversion data through a filter.\\
\textbf{Sampling:} & The term typically refers to the entire process of sampling analog inputs, converting the sampled data, and transferring the conversion results to memory. In certain contexts and stages of the process, sampling may also refer to the SAR ADC capturing data points from an analog signal.\\
\end{longtable}

\section{Features}

The SAR ADCs have the following features:

\begin{itemize}
\item Support HP ADC\regindex{x} controllers and LP ADC\regindex{x} controllers to get control of SAR ADC\regindex{x} via software, \regindex{x} = 1, 2
\item 12-bit resolution
\item Analog inputs sampling from up to 14 pins
\item HP ADC\regindex{x} controllers:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Provide multi-channel sampling control module, supporting multi-channel sampling mode, with configurable channel sampling sequence
        \item Provide mode control module, supporting dual HP ADC sampling
        \item Provide two filters with configurable filter coefficients
        \item Provide two threshold monitors that can trigger an interrupt when the filtered data is above a high threshold or below a low threshold
        \item Support continuous transfer of conversion results to memory via the GDMA interface
    \end{itemize}
\item LP ADC\regindex{x} controllers:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Support one-shot sampling mode
        \item Support sampling in sleep mode (e.g., Deep-sleep)
    \end{itemize}
\item Support for several Event Task Matrix (ETM) related events and tasks
\end{itemize}

    \item Timer: Dedicated timer for the HP ADC\regindex{x} controllers to generate sampling enable signal.
    \item HP ADC FSM\regindex{x}: FSM1 and FSM2 that can:

            \item receive the sampling enable signal from the timer.
            \item determine the conversion rules defined in the patterns for HP ADC\regindex{x}.
            \item drive the HP Reader\regindex{x} module to read conversion data.
            \item transfer conversion data to the filter.
        \end{itemize}
    \item Filter: Filter0 and filter1, used for filtering conversion results in multi-channel sampling mode.
    \item Threshold Monitor: Threshold monitor 0 and threshold monitor 1 that trigger an interrupt when the filtered data is above a high threshold or below a low threshold.

    \item LP Reader\regindex{x}: LP reader 1 and LP reader 2, driven by LP ADC\regindex{x} Controller to read data from LP ADC\regindex{x}.
    \item LP ADC\regindex{x} Controller: LP ADC1 Controller and LP ADC2 Controller. The controllers provide sampling enable signal, drive the LP Reader\regindex{x} module to read the conversion results of LP ADC\regindex{x}, and store the conversion results.
\end{itemize}

\section{Functional Description}

\subsection{SAR ADC Power Up}

The SAR ADCs can be powered up by setting \hyperref[fielddesc:PMUXPDPERIFI2C]{PMU\_XPD\_PERIF\_I2C} and \hyperref[fielddesc:PMUPERIFI2CRST]{PMU\_PERIF\_I2C\_RST}. The ADC sampling can be conducted immediately after power-up. Users need not be concerned about wait time, as it has been accounted for in the hardware design.

\subsection{SAR ADC Clock}

The clock for the HP ADC\regindex{x} Controller has three possible sources, selected by \hyperref[fielddesc:HPSYSCLKRSTADCCLKSRCSEL]{HP\_SYS\_CLKRST\_ADC\_CLK\_SRC\_SEL}:

    \begin{itemize}
       \item XTAL\_CLK
       \item RC\_FAST\_CLK
       \item PLL\_F80M\_CLK
    \end{itemize}

The following clocks can be divided from the HP ADC\regindex{x} Controller's clock:

\begin{itemize}
    \item HPADC\_SARCLK: It is the operating clock of HP ADC\regindex{x} and HP Reader\regindex{x}.
    \item HPADC\_CLK: It is the operating clock of HP ADC FSM\regindex{x}.
\end{itemize}

The frequency of HPADC\_SARCLK can affect the sampling accuracy. If the HPADC\_SARCLK frequency is higher than 5 MHz, the sampling accuracy will decrease. HPADC\_SARCLK is divided from HPADC\_CLK via a dedicated divider. The division is set in \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCSARCLKDIV]{ADC\_SAR\_CLK\_DIV}.

It takes 25 HPADC\_SARCLK clock cycles to complete one sampling, so the maximum sampling rate is limited by the frequency of HPADC\_SARCLK.

The LP ADC\regindex{x} Controller is clocked from LP\_DYN\_FAST\_CLK.

The following clocks can be divided from the LP ADC\regindex{x} Controller's clock:

\begin{itemize}
    \item LPADC\_SARCLK: It is the operating clock of LP ADC\regindex{x} and LP Reader\regindex{x}. The division set in \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1CLKDIV]{LPADC\_SAR1\_CLK\_DIV} and \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR2CLKDIV]{LPADC\_SAR2\_CLK\_DIV} should be at least 2. The frequency of LPADC\_SARCLK should not exceed 5 MHz.
    \item LPADC\_CLK: It is the operating clock of LP ADC FSM\regindex{x}.
\end{itemize}

For more information about clocks, please refer to Chapter \ref{mod:resclk} \textit{\nameref{mod:resclk}}.

\subsection{SAR ADC Conversion and Attenuation}

The SAR ADCs can measure analog voltages from 0 mV to \(V_{ref}\). \(V_{ref}\) is the SAR ADCs' internal reference voltage. The conversion result (\(data\)) is a 12-bit digital value, which is the raw data. To calculate the voltage \(V_{data}\) based on the raw data, the following formula can be used:

    \[
    V_{data} = \frac{V_{ref}}{4095}\times{data}
    \]

\(k\) is the coefficient corresponding to the configured attenuation.

To convert voltages larger than \(V_{ref}\), apply attenuation to the input signals. The supported attenuation levels are:

\begin{itemize}
    \item 0 dB (k≈100\%)
    \item 2.5 dB (k≈75\%)
    \item 6 dB (k≈50\%)
    \item 12 dB (k≈25\%)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{HP ADC FSM}

In multi-channel sampling mode HP ADC FSM (hereinafter referred to as FSM) generates all types of signals used in the sampling process. Figure \ref{fig:sensor-diagram-dig-adc-fsm} illustrates how the FSM works.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.65\textwidth]{\modulefiles/figures/SAR_ADC_FSM}
    \caption{HP ADC FSM Block Diagram}
    \label{fig:sensor-diagram-dig-adc-fsm}
\end{figure}

Wherein:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Timer: a dedicated timer for the HP ADC controller to generate \texttt{sample\_start} signal.
    \item pr: a pointer to the pattern table that defines the conversion rules for HP ADC. FSM sends out corresponding signals based on the conversion rules.
\end{itemize}

Set \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCTIMEREN]{ADC\_TIMER\_EN} to enable the timer. The timeout event of this timer triggers a \texttt{sample\_start} signal. This signal drives the FSM module to start sampling. When the FSM module receives the \texttt{sample\_start} signal, it starts the following operations:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Powers up HP ADC.
    \item Determines the conversion rules (selected sample channels and attenuations) defined in the patterns that the current pr points to.
    \item Outputs the \texttt{en\_pin} and \texttt{atten} signals corresponding to the conversion rules to the analog side.
    \item Initiates the \texttt{sar\_start} signal and starts sampling.
\end{itemize}

When FSM receives the \texttt{reader\_done} signal from HP Reader, it starts the following operations:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Stops sampling.
        \item Transfers the conversion result to the filter. Then the threshold monitor transfers the filtered result to memory via GDMA (see Figure \ref{fig:sar-adc-functional-overview}).
        \item Updates pr and waits for the next sampling. The pointer pr counts cyclically between 0 and ADC\_SAR1/2\_PATT\_LEN (table\_length).
    \end{itemize}

Assuming you want to implement the following sequence of HP ADC1 multi-channel sampling:

\begin{itemize}

    \item Sample channel 0 of HP ADC1 with attenuation of 2.5 dB.
\end{itemize}

Then, the detailed configuration is as follows:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Configure the first pattern (cmd0) of \hyperref[regdesc:ADCSAR1PATTTAB1REG]{ADC\_SAR1\_PATT\_TAB1\_REG}[5:0]:

    \item [ch\_sel] write 2 to this field, to select channel 2.
    \end{reglist}\end{regdesc}
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Configure the second pattern (cmd1) of \hyperref[regdesc:ADCSAR1PATTTAB1REG]{ADC\_SAR1\_PATT\_TAB1\_REG}[11:6]:

    \item Enable the timer so that the HP ADC1 Controller starts sampling the two channels periodically.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Dual HP ADC Sampling Control}

\hyperref[fielddesc:ADCWORKMODE]{ADC\_WORK\_MODE} controls how two HP ADCs work together:

\begin{itemize}
    \item 0: Single HP ADC sampling, i.e., only one HP ADC samples. When a sampling request is triggered by the timer, \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCSARSEL]{ADC\_SAR\_SEL} controls which FSM receives the sampling request:

        \begin{itemize}
            \item 0: HP ADC FSM1, i.e., using HP ADC1 for sampling.
            \item 1: HP ADC FSM2, i.e., using HP ADC2 for sampling.
        \end{itemize}

    \item 1: Dual HP ADC simultaneous sampling. When a sampling request is triggered by the timer, both HP ADC FSM1 and HP ADC FSM2 receive the sampling request.
    \item 2: Dual HP ADC alternative sampling. When an odd number of sampling requests are triggered by the timer, HP ADC FSM1 receives the sampling request. When an even number of sampling requests are triggered by the timer, HP ADC FSM2 receives the sampling request.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{HP ADC Filters}\label{sec:adc_filter}

The HP ADC\regindex{x} controllers provide two filters for filtering conversion results in multi-channel sampling mode. Both filters can be configured to any HP ADC channel, but cannot be configured to the same channel. If the two filters are configured to the same channel, the first one takes effect.

The filtered data is determined by the following equation:

\[data_{cur} = \frac{(k-1)data_{prev}}{k}+\frac{data_{in}}{k}+0.5\]

\begin{itemize}
    \item \(data_{cur}\): the filtered data
    \item \(data_{in}\): the conversion result
    \item \(data_{prev}\): the last filtered data
    \item \(k\): the filter coefficient
\end{itemize}

The filters are configured as follows:

\begin{itemize}
\item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCFILTERCHANNEL0]{ADC\_FILTER\_CHANNEL\regindex{x}} to select the HP ADC channel for filter \regindex{x}, \regindex{x}=0, 1.
\item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCFILTERFACTOR0]{ADC\_FILTER\_FACTOR\regindex{x}} to set the coefficient \(k\) for filter \regindex{x}, \regindex{x}=0, 1.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{HP ADC Threshold Monitors}\label{sec:adc_threshold}

The HP ADC\regindex{x} controllers provide two threshold monitors to monitor the filtered data in multi-channel sampling mode. When the data is above the high threshold, a high threshold interrupt is triggered; when the data is below the low threshold, a low threshold interrupt is triggered. Both monitors can be configured to any HP ADC channel, but cannot be configured to the same channel.

Threshold monitors are configured as follows:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCTHRES0EN]{ADC\_THRES\regindex{x}\_EN} to enable threshold monitor \regindex{x} (\regindex{x}=0, 1).
    \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCTHRES0LOW]{ADC\_THRES\regindex{x}\_LOW} to set a low threshold.
    \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCTHRES0LOW]{ADC\_THRES\regindex{x}\_HIGH} to set a high threshold.
    \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCTHRES0LOW]{ADC\_THRES\regindex{x}\_CHANNEL} to select the channel to monitor.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{LP ADC Wake-Up Modes for Automatic Monitoring}

The LP ADC\regindex{x} controllers support automatic monitoring of the conversion data. A dedicated timer generates LP ADC sampling signals periodically based on the value of \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCADC1HWREADENI]{LPADC\_ADC\regindex{x}\_HW\_READ\_RATE\_I}. Then the conversion data is fed into a threshold monitor (not the HP ADC threshold monitors). If the data exceeds the threshold, an interrupt will be generated.

There are two wake-up modes for automatic monitoring, selected by \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPMODE]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_MODE}:

   \begin{itemize}
       \item 0: Absolute value mode \\
        Monitors the absolute value of the conversion data. Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPTHLOW]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_TH\_LOW} and \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPTHHIGH]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_TH\_HIGH} to set the thresholds. Wake-up will be triggered if the data is above the high threshold or below the low threshold.

       \item 1: Change value mode \\
        Monitors the changes of the conversion data. If the increment of two consecutive samplings exceeds the high threshold configured in \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPTHHIGH]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_TH\_HIGH}, or the decrement of two consecutive samplings exceeds the low threshold configured in \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPTHLOW]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_TH\_LOW}, a wake-up will be triggered. For example, when \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPTHLOW]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_TH\_LOW} is set to 8, and the two consecutive conversion results are 28 and 19, resulting in a decrement of 9, a wake-up will be triggered.

    \end{itemize}

\section{Event Task Matrix Feature}
\label{sec:adc-etm-func}

The SAR ADC on \chipname{} supports the Event Task Matrix (ETM) function, which allows SAR ADC's ETM tasks to be triggered by any peripherals' ETM events, or SAR ADC's ETM events to trigger any peripherals' ETM tasks. This section introduces the ETM tasks and events related to SAR ADC. For more information, please refer to Chapter \ref{mod:evntaskmatrix} \nameref{mod:evntaskmatrix}.

The SAR ADC can receive the following ETM tasks:

\begin{itemize}
    \item ADC\_TASK\_SAMPLE0: LP ADC starts one-shot sampling when this task is triggered.
    \item ADC\_TASK\_START0: HP ADC starts multi-channel sampling when this task is triggered.
    \item ADC\_TASK\_STOP0: SAR ADC stops sampling when this task is triggered.
\end{itemize}

The SAR ADC can generate the following ETM events:

\begin{itemize}
    \item ADC\_EVT\_CONV\_CMPLT0: Generated each time SAR ADC completes a sampling in either one-shot sampling mode or multi-channel sampling mode.
    \item ADC\_EVT\_EQ\_ABOVE\_THRESH\regindex{x}: Generated when the HP ADC's filtered data is above the threshold. \regindex{x} = 0, 1, representing threshold monitor 0, 1.
    \item ADC\_EVT\_EQ\_BELOW\_THRESH\regindex{x}: Generated when the HP ADC's filtered data is below the threshold. \regindex{x} = 0, 1, representing threshold monitor 0, 1.
    \item ADC\_EVT\_STARTED0: Generated when SAR ADC begins sampling; one-shot sampling will not trigger this event.
    \item ADC\_EVT\_STOPPED0: Generated when SAR ADC stops sampling, one-shot sampling will not trigger this event.
\end{itemize}

In practical applications, SAR ADC's ETM events can trigger its own ETM tasks. For example, the ADC\_EVT\_EQ\_ABOVE\_THRESH\regindex{x} event can trigger the ADC\_TASK\_STOP0 task.

\section{Interrupts}

\chipname{}'s SAR ADC can generate the following interrupt signals that will be sent to the \hyperref[mod:intmtrx]{Interrupt Matrix}.

   \begin{itemize}
        \item ADC\_INTR
        \item LP\_ADC\_INTR
    \end{itemize}

The following internal interrupt sources from SAR ADC can generate the interrupt signal ADC\_INTR:

\begin{itemize}
   \item \label{interreupt:ADCDONEINT1} ADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_DONE\_INT: Triggered when HP ADC\regindex{x} completes one sampling.
   \item \label{interreupt:ADCTHRESxHIGHINT} ADC\_THRES\regindex{x}\_HIGH\_INT: Triggered when the filtered data is above the high threshold.
   \item \label{interreupt:ADCTHRESxLOWINT} ADC\_THRES\regindex{x}\_LOW\_INT: Triggered when the filtered data is below the low threshold.
\end{itemize}

The following internal interrupt sources from SAR ADC can generate the interrupt signal LP\_ADC\_INTR:

\begin{itemize}
   \item \label{interreupt:LPADCCOCPUSARADCxINT} LPADC\_COCPU\_SARADC\regindex{x}\_DONE\_INT: Triggered when LP ADC\regindex{x} completes one sampling.
   \item \label{interreupt:LPADCCOCPUSARADCxERRORINT} LPADC\_COCPU\_SARADC\regindex{x}\_ERROR\_INT: Triggered when sampling error occurs.
   \item \label{interreupt:LPADCCOCPUSARADCxWAKEINT} LPADC\_COCPU\_SARADC\regindex{x}\_WAKE\_INT: Triggered when LP ADC\regindex{x} generates a wake-up event (such as conversion data exceeding the threshold).
\end{itemize}

Each interrupt source can be configured by a common set of registers that are described in Section \hyperref[interrupt-config-registers]{\textit{Interrupt Configuration Registers}}. The specific registers can be found in Section \ref{sec:adc-reg-summ} \textit{\nameref{sec:adc-reg-summ}}.

\section{Programming Procedure}

\subsection{HP ADC Multi-Channel Sampling Mode}

The multi-channel sampling mode can be configured with the following procedure:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:PMUXPDPERIFI2C]{PMU\_XPD\_PERIF\_I2C} and \hyperref[fielddesc:PMUPERIFI2CRST]{PMU\_PERIF\_I2C\_RST} to power up HP ADC.
    \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:HPSYSCLKRSTADCCLKSRCSEL]{HP\_SYS\_CLKRST\_ADC\_CLK\_SRC\_SEL} to select HP ADC clock source.
    \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:HPSYSCLKRSTADCCLKDIVNUM]{HP\_SYS\_CLKRST\_ADC\_CLK\_DIV\_NUM} and \hyperref[fielddesc:HPSYSCLKRSTADCSARCLKDIVNUM]{HP\_SYS\_CLKRST\_ADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_CLK\_DIV\_NUM} to set clock division.
    \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:HPSYSCLKRSTADCCLKEN]{HP\_SYS\_CLKRST\_ADC\_CLK\_EN} to enable HP ADC clock.
    \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1DIGFORCE]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_DIG\_FORCE} to enable the sampling channel.
    \item Configure the pattern table as described in Section \ref{sec:adc_pattern} \textit{\nameref{sec:adc_pattern}}.
    \item Configure channels and filtering coefficients as described in Section \ref{sec:adc_filter} \textit{\nameref{sec:adc_filter}}.
    \item Configure threshold monitoring as described in Section \ref{sec:adc_threshold} \textit{\nameref{sec:adc_threshold}}, as needed.
    \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCAPBADCTRANS]{ADC\_APB\_ADC\_TRANS} to use GDMA.
    \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCTIMERTARGET]{ADC\_TIMER\_TARGET} to set trigger target for HP ADC timer.
    \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCTIMEREN]{ADC\_TIMER\_EN} to enable the timer.
\end{enumerate}

The timer timeout will trigger HP ADC FSM to start sampling according to the pattern table. The conversion result will be automatically stored in memory. When the sampling reaches the specified limit in  \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCAPBADCEOFNUM]{ADC\_APB\_ADC\_EOF\_NUM}, it will terminate.

Once sampling is complete, an \hyperref[fielddesc:ADCSAR1DONEINTRAW]{ADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_DONE\_INT\_RAW} interrupt will be generated.

\subsection{LP ADC One-shot Sampling Mode}

The one-shot sampling mode can be configured with the following procedure:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:PMUXPDPERIFI2C]{PMU\_XPD\_PERIF\_I2C} and \hyperref[fielddesc:PMUPERIFI2CRSTB]{PMU\_PERIF\_I2C\_RSTB} to power up LP ADC.
    \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:LPPERILPADCFUNCDIVNUM]{LPPERI\_LPADC\_FUNC\_DIV\_NUM} and \hyperref[fielddesc:LPPERILPADCSAR1DIVNUM]{LPPERI\_LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_DIV\_NUM} to set the frequency of LPADC\_CLK and LPADC\regindex{x}\_SARCLK.
    \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1ENPAD]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_EN\_PAD} and \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1ATTEN]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_ATTEN} to select sampling channels and attenuation.
    \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCMEAS1STARTSAR]{LPADC\_SAR\_MEAS\regindex{x}\_START\_SAR} to enable the one-shot sampling mode of LP ADC\regindex{x}.
\end{enumerate}

Once sampling is complete, an \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCCOCPUSARADC1INTRAW]{LPADC\_COCPU\_SARADC\regindex{x}\_INT\_RAW} interrupt is generated. Software can read the conversion result from \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCMEAS1DATASAR]{LPADC\_SAR\_MEAS\regindex{n}\_DATA\_SAR}.

\subsection{LP ADC Automatic Monitoring}

The configuration process for the automatic monitoring of the LP ADC controller is as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
        \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCADC1HWREADRATEI]{LPADC\_ADC\regindex{x}\_HW\_READ\_RATE\_I} to set the sampling rate.
        \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPMODE]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_MODE} to select the wake-up mode.
        \item Configure \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPTHHIGH]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_TH\_HIGH} and \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPTHLOW]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_TH\_LOW} to set the high and low thresholds.
        \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCSAR1WAKEUPEN]{LPADC\_SAR\regindex{x}\_WAKEUP\_EN} to enable automatic monitoring.
        \item Set \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCADC1HWREADENI]{LPADC\_ADC\regindex{x}\_HW\_READ\_EN\_I} to enable periodic monitoring.
\end{enumerate}

During the automatic monitoring process, the conversion data will not be stored, but software can always read the last conversion data from \hyperref[fielddesc:LPADCMEAS1DATASAR]{LPADC\_SAR\_MEAS\regindex{n}\_DATA\_SAR}.
